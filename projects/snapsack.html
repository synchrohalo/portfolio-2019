Snapsack

I was entrusted to create a cloud storage app, with the task of discovering in what ways a new competitor could enter the cloud storage app market. The result was an app similar to Google Drive and Dropbox, but with the added functionality of labels and a warmer, friendlier interface.

Summary

Roles

UX Designer, UI Designer, Illustrator

Tools

Paper & pen, Figma, Photoshop, Illustrator, Krita Paint

Deliverables

User surveys, Competitive analysis, User personas, User stories, User flows, Sitemap, Low-fidelity wireframes, High-fidelity mockups, Branding guide, Graphic illustration

The problem

Upon learning that users were not likely to sort their files into folders despite finding organization an important feature for cloud storage apps, I sought to find the answer to the question, how can cloud storage platforms be improved such that users will be more likely keep their digital spaces organized?

The solution

Enter Snapsack, a multi-purpose cloud storage app with a focus on organization. A labeling system allows users to visually categorize their files and folders at a glance. Filtering gives users the ability to sift through files that may be buried deep within nested folders or see all types of files and folders associated with a label.

Process

1. Research

User survey

I created a survey to learn about cloud storage apps and their users. I asked questions about which apps participants use and which features they found most important and used most frequently.

I found myself fixated on the last point. It seemed that users were out of the habit of keeping their digital spaces organized.

Competitive analysis

To scope out the competition I selected two cloud storage platforms I was familiar with: Google Drive and Dropbox.

Takeaways

The two function similarly, with the main differentiating factor being that Dropbox sees itself as a productivity tool rather than a storage app. Furthermore, the only way to organize files is by moving them between folders (and by selecting folder colors in Drive).

Could I improve a cloud storage app in terms of organization? This discovery was pertinent to my growing interest in how to encourage people to organize their digital spaces.

User stories

The most impactful user stories I came up with, in order of priority (based on what people use frequently and rated important) and relevance, would make up the MVP of my product:

Minimum viable product

  • Upload individual files

  • Upload a group of files from my computer and mobile device

  • Access and recover deleted files

  • Create a document from scratch

  • Leave comments on files

  • Organize my files in folders or by tags/labels

  • Share a file with others

  • View the amount of available storage space left

User flows

It was during this stage that I played around more with the idea of labels. I designed my flows under the assumption that folders were a limiting way of navigation and that some sort of labeling or tagging system could help solve two problems that I had with folders:

Problems with folders

  1. Hard to access files deeply nested within many folders.

  2. Can only associate a file with one folder and its encapsulating folders.

I also thought about the benefits of using color-coded labels:

Benefits of labels

  1. Provide visual cues with the use of color.

  2. Files/folders can be associated with more than one label.

I drafted the following user flows based on the the labelling system developing in my mind. The idea was to used color-coded labels so that users could have a visual cue with which to identify the files and folders on their app.

Wireframe user testing

I tested my wireframes with 3 participants, which revealed one commonality -- that the labeling interactions needed some work.

Grouping by association

Filter by label is associated with searching and should be grouped with actions similar to it.

Law of proximity

Editing the labels on a file takes the user to the other side of the screen, which is too far from the the selected file. Associated items and actions

Scalability
