Ice cream delivery app

Between June 4 to June 14, 2019, I attempted the Adobe XD Daily Creative challenge to familiarize myself with the program. The brief for this circuit was to design the UI for a food delivery app, similar to Caviar, Postmates, and the like. I chose to focus my app on ice cream delivery under the name Jingle.

Summary

Team

Solo project

Roles

UI Designer, Illustrator

Tools

Paper & pen, Adobe XD

Deliverables

High-fidelity mockups, Graphic illustration, Simple branding guide

Process

Challenge 01

Home Screen

From day one, I decided to design the app for the iPhone X. The first prompt was to create an engaging home screen, given that new users spend less than 30 seconds on it after logging in for the first time.

Solution

I used large images and highlighted deals to entice the user to take action.

Challenge 02

Order History

The next step was to create a screen for past orders while making sure it is easily accessible from the home screen.

Solution

The user can access the Orders screen in two ways: By going to their profile or by accessing it from the Order it again section on the Home screen. As for the screen itself, I made a simple list with expandable items to view orders in detail. I took the opportunity to create a simple ice cream illustration to make the screen pop.

Challenge 03

Search & Filter

How do you find an ice cream shop in the first place? The prompt called for participants to design an experience that would help users quickly filter through their options.

Solution

I highlighted classic ice cream flavors in a visual way for a faster way to search. The user also has the option to type into a search bar and/or toggle filters to narrow down their options.

Challenge 04

Marketing Priorities

The challenge for the day was to prioritize the app’s current marketing campaigns.

Solution

Following the convention used by similar apps, I created a space for promotions at the top of the Home screen. I made up my own campaigns, including this Filipino ice cream promotion pictured here, to exercise my illustration skills.

Challenge 05

Place Order

The meat of the app. The goal was to simplify the ordering process as users tend to add items to their cart without actually following through with their orders.

Solution

I tried to make ordering easier for the user by keeping visual cues clear throughout the process. For instance, users should be able to tell whether or not they’ve chosen all of the required options for an item before they can add it to their cart. The app’s primary color is used to indicate that the cart is filled and that the user can now place the order.

Challenge 06

Address Alert

Suppose the user has delivered to the wrong address. What can they do to fix this problem with minimal frustration?

Solution

I added some text to let the user know that they have until a certain time to make edits to their order. If all else fails, a link to a contact page is included on the Track your order screen if further assistance is needed.

Challenge 07

Order Status

Challenge #7 asked participants to design screens for users to track their orders in real time. I had unintentionally completed this in the previous two challenges.

Challenge 08

Delivery Map

Challenge #8 asked participants for a map that shows the location and arrival time of their food. I had also already completed this in the previous challenge.

Revisions

I worked on the app incrementally by screen, making big and small changes along the way. The most apparent change can be seen in the transformation of the Home screen.

Nav bar

Originally the nav bar was more visually-striking. I was hoping to draw attention to the bar to encourage users to start the delivery process. As I continued working through the app, however, I decided to keep the emphasis on the businesses themselves, making the ice cream photos the focus of the screen.

Color palette

Because I wanted the app to feel fun, I ended up using too many colors, which made it hard to balance my palette. I updated the app with a more controlled palette with a modest use of saturated colors.

Feedback

Following my completion of the challenges, I tested my app with two users to gain insight on what could be improved.

Emphasize headings

A piece of advice I got was that emphasizing my headings would help with visually separating the sections in my app. I did so by bolding the heading text to increase its visual weight.

Encourage action

One of my testers found that there was no obvious action to take on the Home screen and suggested having past orders closer to the top because the familiarity would make them more likely to order again. As such, I moved Get it again to the top.

Add time to change order

A tester thought that it would be more helpful to show the time by which they could edit their order on the order information screen itself, rather than on the tracking screen.

Branding

On the first day of the challenge I came up with the name Jingle, a reference to the chimes that play as an ice cream truck approaches. The simple pseudo logo depicts a bell-shaped scoop of ice cream on a cone.

Color palette

  • Hex: #E0517E

  • Hex: #FFDAD3

  • Hex: #BEE4FF

  • Hex: #FFFFFF

  • Hex: #3E3232

  • Hex: #776868

Warm pinks and browns are the primary app colors, reminiscent of strawberry ice cream on a cone. A light, icy blue serves as contrast and relieves the eyes with a cool feeling.

Type

  • Fugaz One - A fun, decorative font that has summer vibes (not that ice cream is only to be eaten in hot weather, of course).

  • Karla - A grotesque sans serif for some personality.

Conclusion

This was a fun, quick project that helped me get accustomed to Adobe XD (which, at this time of writing, is now my prototyping tool of choice). One of the highlights was getting to experiment with the powerful Auto Animate feature, which may have piqued my interest in UI animation.

What I learned

  • Version control - Because it was a quick project that I modified fairly often, I failed to save some of the previous versions I'd made for comparison. In the future, I need to come up with a workflow that incorporates some form of version control.

  • Emphasize headings - Upon evaluating my own work, I couldn’t put my finger on why some of my sections were difficult to tell apart. A peer reviewing my type choices pointed out that enlarging and adding weight to the headings would help. I followed her advice with stellar results! It reminded me not to overlook simple solutions that make use of basic design principles.
